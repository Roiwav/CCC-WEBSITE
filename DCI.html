About Us About CCC

VISION

A leader provider of excellent information technology professional who manifest global standards, prepare to meet and respond to the needs and demands of the computer and IT industry.

MISSION

To strengthen the foundation for computer and IT education and literacy by exposing the would-be IT professionals to the theories and practice guided by ethics, values and professionalism.

PROGRAMS OFFERED

Bachelor of Science in Information Technology

  • Program Outcomes
    • Knowledge for Solving Computing Problems:
      IT01 - Apply knowledge of computing, science, and mathematics appropriate to the discipline.
      IT02 - Understand best practices and standards and their applications.
    • Problem Analysis:
      IT03 - Analyze complex problems and define computing requirements.
      IT04 - Identify and analyze user needs in the creation of computer-based systems.
    • Design/Development of Solutions:
      IT05 - Design, implement, and evaluate computer-based systems to meet requirements.
      IT06 - Integrate IT solutions into the user environment effectively.
    • Modern Tool Usage:
      IT07 - Apply knowledge through the use of current techniques and tools necessary for the IT profession.
    • Communication:
      IT10 - Communicate effectively with the computing community and society at large through logical writing, presentations, and clear instructions.
    • Computing Professionalism and Ethics:
      IT11 - Analyze the global impact of IT on society.
      IT12 - Understand professional, ethical, legal, and social responsibilities.
    • Life-Long Learning:
      IT13 - Engage in self-learning for continuing professional development.

Bachelor of Science in Computer Science

  • Program Outcomes
    • Knowledge for Solving Computing Problems:
      CS01 - Apply knowledge of computing fundamentals and mathematics appropriate to the computing specialization.
    • Problem Analysis:
      CS02 - Solve complex computing problems using mathematics, computing sciences, and relevant disciplines.
    • Design/Development of Solutions:
      CS03 - Apply mathematical foundations and algorithmic principles in system modeling and design.
    • Modern Tool Usage:
      CS06 - Apply modern computing tools to complex computing activities.
    • Communication:
      CS08 - Communicate effectively about complex computing activities through reports, presentations, and instructions.
    • Computing Professionalism and Ethics:
      CS09 - Recognize the ethical and legal issues in utilizing computer technology.
    • Life-Long Learning:
      CS10 - Engage in continuous self-learning for professional growth.
